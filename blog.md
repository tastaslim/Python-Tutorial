# Druva Interview Experience for SDE1 | Off-Campus | 2021  #
There is hardly any interview experience for the SDE-1 profile at Druva, So, It is my responsibility to write this one. I hope it will give you a glimpse of it and help you.
I have 5 months of experience in Software development. I got a call through referral program in October from Druva Hyderabad. 
I will share my journey from referral to interview in this post.

---

## How did I get a referral at Druva ##
Since Druva doesn't hire freshers often and I am a student of Tier3 college, it was difficult for me to get a referral here. Before asking for a referral, I would suggest going through the job profile and checking whether it matches your skills or not. I sent a connection request to 50+ Druva employees on LinkedIn, interacted with them, got know some stats and insights of Druva, and asked for a referral. 

--- 

## Message format to ask for referral ##
While asking someone for a referral please make sure, you provide them with appropriate information about your skills and job profile. Most people don't get a reply because they don't provide proper information to other people. Generally, I follow below format:
   - Brief Introduction ( Name, education, company, and skills)
   - Job Id/link for which you are asking referral.
   - Updated Resume( With best projects, skills, and reference to multiple coding platforms like Leetcode, Codechef, GeeksForGeeks, etc.) 

---

## Interviews ##
There was 1 online test followed by 2 technical and 1 hiring manager round.
1. **Round 1(Online Test: Time - 70 minutes):**
This round was consist of 3 sections:
   - 2 problem-solving questions based on Dynamic programming.
   - 5 MCQs on Data Structures such as Heap, Tree, Stack, Queue, etc. 
   - 5 MCQs on SQL.
I solved 1.5 (1st complete and 2nd partially) out of 2 problems solving, 5-5 DS and SQL MCQs section.

---

2. **Round 2(F2F Technical – Time: 1 hr):**
After 2 days of the online test, I got a call from a recruiter that I passed the online test and my interviews will be scheduled. My first technical interview was scheduled on Zoom and was taken by Engineering Manager. There were two Leetcode medium/hard problem questions based on Binary Tree (Variation of zigzag traversal) and variation of Kadane's algorithm. I don't remember the exact questions, but I will share the standard one's on which questions were made.

   -  [Problem1: Zigzag traversal of binary tree](https://leetcode.com/problems/binary-tree-zigzag-level-order-traversal/)

   - [Problem2: Maximum sum sub-array in a circular array](https://leetcode.com/problems/maximum-sum-circular-subarray/)

   I explained the approach to both questions and wrote optimized code. The interviewer was very friendly and gave me hints wherever I was stuck. Overall, it was a great round and the interviewer was satisfied with my solutions.

   ---

   3. **Round 3(F2F Technical – Time: 1 hr):**

   After 3 days of the first F2F round, my second round was scheduled, which was taken by Principal Engineer. This round was purely based on my projects and technical skills. He asked me questions related to my domain, projects in-depth on various factors such as the scope of project, scalability, fault tolerance, Rest APIs, various OAuth2 flows, etc. and a few questions related to the Operating System. 
   After this, he gave a medium level coding question which was based on heap and string. I explained the approach of the solution and the interviewer was satisfied with it.
   
   [Rearrange characters in a string such that no two adjacent are same](https://www.geeksforgeeks.org/rearrange-characters-string-no-two-adjacent/)

   ---

   4. **Round 4(F2F Hiring Manager - Time: 1 hr):**

   The last round was Hiring Manager. It was scheduled on the same day after 2nd technical round and was taken by the Director of Engineering. The interviewer was pretty cool and exchanged pleasantries with me.
 He asked me questions related to my projects, experience, and work in the previous company, family background. Since I was experienced in the same domain in which the company works, we discussed the importance of data backup, security, my understanding of SAAS products such as Salesforce, Microsoft365, GSuit, my expectations from Druva, etc.

   ---

## Tips ##

1. You must know how to calculate time and space complexities.
2. In each round they ask you about the project you recently did/ the project you liked working on most/ most challenging work etc. So you should be prepared well for at least one project with in-depth details.
3. Start with the naive approach for each question asked and then proceed with solutions with better space and time complexities.
4. Don't jump on solution directly, discuss your approach and ask questions for corner test cases.


**Verdict:** Selected, I accepted the offer. I thank Druva for the opportunity and my recruiter for the smooth process and onboarding.
